# AI Consulting Business Roadmap — Ground Up, Internal & External

**Read this first — the strategic frame.** You're the Rainmaker. That's the single biggest predictor of consulting success. The market is wide open: in 2026, ~88% of organizations use AI somewhere, but only ~1% are "AI-mature," and roughly 30% of GenAI pilots got abandoned — the *entire* SMB market needs help and a chunk of it already failed once and will pay a premium for someone who can make attempt #2 work. Your risk isn't sales; it's delivery. This roadmap is engineered around that reality: sell what you can reliably deliver, productize ruthlessly, and use co-founders or subcontractors for execution.

**How this relates to CloudGuard:** decide consciously. AI consulting can be (a) a standalone business, (b) a cash-flow engine that funds CloudGuard, or (c) the top-of-funnel — engagements surface the exact security pain CloudGuard sells into. Run it as **(b)+(c)**.

**The order of operations principle:** Niche → Offer → Proof → Pipeline → Sales → Delivery → Systemize → Scale. You do **not** form an LLC and build a fancy website on day one.

## PHASE 0 — Foundation & Positioning (Weeks 1–3)

### 1. Pick a niche (the highest-leverage decision you'll make)
Generalist "AI consultant" is commoditizing fast. The defensible money is in **vertical specialization** (legal, healthcare, real estate, professional services) and **moving up the value chain** (implementation + governance + ROI accountability, not just advice).

For you the obvious wedge is **DMV-area professional-services firms, especially law firms** — reuse the CloudGuard ICP (10–75 attorneys, high data sensitivity, AI-curious). Pick **one** vertical + **one** core problem. "I help small DMV law firms automate intake and document workflows without putting client data at risk" beats "I do AI for businesses."

### 2. Define your offer ladder (productize from day one)
Don't sell hours; sell outcomes with fixed scope:
- **AI Readiness Assessment** — $2,000–$8,000, 2–4 weeks. Audit ops, identify 3–5 high-ROI use cases, deliver a prioritized roadmap with estimated ROI. *Your foot-in-the-door product.*
- **AI Strategy & Roadmap** — $8,000–$25,000, 4–8 weeks. Tool selection, integration plan, phased rollout, vendor eval.
- **Pilot Implementation** — $15,000+. Build and deploy one high-value workflow.
- **Monthly Retainer** — $5,000–$15,000/mo. *This is the goal — recurring revenue.* Always tie retainers to concrete monthly deliverables.

Lead with the **Assessment** — cheap for them, fast for you, low delivery risk. The assessment *is* the sales process for the bigger engagements.

### 3. Set your pricing & model
2026 benchmarks: solo/independent consultants run **$80–$200/hr**, boutiques $150–$300. Quote fixed-fee per the tiers so margin scales with speed, not time. Note for later: **AI governance / compliance** ($300–$600/hr) is the fastest-growing, highest-paying specialization. **Avoid the traps:** no 100%-upfront, no vague "strategic guidance" retainers, no discovery phases that drag for months. Disclose tool/API costs upfront.

### 4. The build-vs-sell honesty check
You sell; you don't deliver technical builds. Decide your delivery model *now*: co-founders, a subcontractor bench, or the no/low-code tier you can ship yourself (n8n, Make, Claude API, Supabase). **Never sell beyond your delivery capacity.** A blown delivery in a tight vertical poisons your referral network.

## PHASE 1 — Legal, Financial & Operational Setup (Weeks 2–4, in parallel)

### 5. Legal entity
- Form an **LLC** (get the operating agreement right if partners are involved, especially re: CloudGuard's cap table). In Virginia, register with the State Corporation Commission.
- Get an **EIN** from the IRS (free, online, 10 minutes).
- Consider an **S-corp election** later once profit justifies it — not day one.

### 6. Banking & finance
- Separate **business checking account** (never commingle).
- **Bookkeeping** from transaction #1 (spreadsheet or QuickBooks/Wave).
- Understand **quarterly estimated taxes** — set aside ~25–30% of profit.
- A simple **CPA relationship** once revenue is real.

### 7. Contracts & risk protection (critical for law-firm clients)
- A solid **Master Services Agreement + Statement of Work** template. SOW defines scope, deliverables, timeline, payment milestones, and what's *out* of scope.
- **Mutual NDA** template (law firms will require it).
- **Data Processing Agreement / confidentiality terms** — non-negotiable for legal clients. Where your CloudGuard knowledge makes you credible.
- **Professional liability (E&O) insurance** — before your first real engagement.
- Payment terms: deposit upfront (30–50%), milestone billing, net-15.

### 8. Internal tech & ops stack
- **CRM** — reuse your React/Vite/Supabase build or HubSpot free tier + Clay for enrichment.
- **Project/delivery management** — Notion or Linear; a repeatable engagement template.
- **Comms & scheduling** — professional email, Calendly, a video tool.
- **Delivery tooling** — your existing n8n/Make/Claude API/Supabase stack.
- **Document/proposal tooling** — proposal templates, an assessment-report template.

## PHASE 2 — Brand, Proof & First Clients (Weeks 3–10)

The goal: **one to three paying clients and the proof assets that make the next ten easier.**

### 9. Minimum viable brand
- **Name + simple logo + one-page website.** Who you help, the problem you solve, your offer tiers, proof, a clear CTA to book a call.
- **LinkedIn positioned around the niche.**
- **A case-study/portfolio page** — empty at first; fill it after every engagement.

### 10. Manufacture proof before you have clients
- **Build 2–3 demo/sample deliverables** — a sample AI readiness assessment, a working intake-automation demo, a RAG assistant over public legal docs.
- **Do 1–2 free or steeply-discounted pilots** for a friendly business in exchange for a testimonial + case study + referral.
- **Publish proof-of-expertise content** — short LinkedIn posts/demos.

### 11. Lead generation — run multiple channels, double down on what hits
- **Warm network + referrals first.** Cheapest, highest-converting.
- **Targeted cold outreach** (your strength). Use the CloudGuard ICP + Clay. Personalized, problem-led. Offer the low-risk Assessment as the CTA.
- **Upwork / freelance platforms** as a starter pipeline — build proof, then graduate off it.
- **Content / inbound** (compounding, slower). Niche posts + a lead magnet ("AI Readiness Checklist for Law Firms").
- **Partnerships / referral channels** — MSPs, IT providers, bar associations, accountants serving the same firms.

### 12. Sales process (productize this like everything else)
1. **Discovery call** — diagnose, don't pitch. Find the expensive, painful, repetitive problem. Qualify budget and decision-maker.
2. **Proposal** — tie to *their* ROI in dollars/hours saved. Three options (good/better/best) anchors higher.
3. **Close** — handle objections (cost, data risk, "can't we just use ChatGPT?"). Your data-security fluency crushes the risk objection.
4. **Onboard** — signed SOW, deposit, kickoff. Set expectations hard on scope and timeline.

## PHASE 3 — Delivery, Retention & Systemization (Month 3+)

### 13. Deliver like a professional
- **A documented delivery methodology** per offer tier — turn each engagement into a repeatable playbook.
- **Communicate relentlessly** — weekly updates, no surprises.
- **Document everything** — every engagement produces a reusable asset.
- **Manage scope ruthlessly** — change requests = change orders = more money.

### 14. Retain and expand (the real business)
- **Land-and-expand:** Assessment → pilot → retainer. Map the upsell path into every engagement.
- **Convert projects to retainers** — recurring revenue is the difference between a job and a business.
- **Referrals as a system** — ask at the moment of delivered value.
- **The CloudGuard bridge** — every implementation engagement surfaces data/security exposure. Consulting becomes CloudGuard's qualified-lead engine.

### 15. Systemize for scale (Month 6+)
- **SOPs for everything** — sales, onboarding, delivery, reporting.
- **Raise rates** as proof accumulates.
- **Build the bench** — productize delivery enough to hand to subcontractors or a first hire.
- **Specialize upward** — migrate from commoditized automation toward governance, compliance, and ROI-accountable implementation.
- **Decide the endgame** — is consulting the business, the funding mechanism, or the lead-gen front end? Revisit deliberately.

## How to not fail this

- **Sell the Assessment first.** Low buyer risk, fast delivery, the on-ramp to everything bigger.
- **Your sales ability is the moat; delivery is the risk.** Never sell past what you can reliably ship.
- **Productize relentlessly.** Hours don't scale; templated offers and SOPs do.
- **Move up the value chain before commoditization catches you.** Governance is your CloudGuard bridge.
- **Recurring revenue is the whole game.** Engineer every engagement toward an ongoing relationship.
- **Keep it in service of the bigger picture.** Decide whether this funds the dream or quietly becomes it — and set the guardrails now.
